Overview

In the current Unification governance implementation anyone can submit a proposal to the system. A minimum deposit is required for a proposal to enter the voting period during which FUND holders will be able to vote on whether the proposal is accepted or not. The numbers used in the following are based on the parameters implemented on the Unification Blockchain.

Phase 1: The Deposit Period

For a proposal to be considered for voting, a minimum deposit of 1,500 FUND needs to be deposited within 1 weeks from when the proposal was submitted. Any FUND holder can contribute to this deposit to support proposals, meaning that the party submitting the proposal doesn’t necessarily need to provide the deposit itself. The deposit is required as spam protection, FUND holders that contributed FUND to a proposal will be able to collect their deposit when the proposal was accepted or when it did not reach the minimum threshold after 2 weeks.

Phase 2: The Voting Period

When the minimum deposit for a particular proposal is reached the 1 week (168h) long voting period begins. During this period, FUND holders are able to cast their vote on that proposal. There are 4 voting options (“Yes”, “No”, “No with Veto”, “Abstain”). Important details in the governance implementation of Unification are:

  • Only staked (bonded) tokens can participate in governance.
  • Voting power is measured in terms of stake. The number of FUND you stake determine your influence on the decision (coin voting).
  • Delegators inherit the vote of the validators they are delegated to unless they cast their own vote, which will overwrite validator decisions.

Phase 3: Tallying Results

If a proposal is accepted depends on the result of the coin voting by FUND holders. The following requirements need to be satisfied for a proposal to be considered accepted:

  • Quorum: More than 40% of the total staked FUND at the end of the voting period need to have participated in the vote.
  • Threshold: More than 50% of FUND that participated in the vote (after excluding “Abstain” votes) need to have voted in favor of the proposal (“Yes”).
  • Veto: Less than 33.4% of the tokens that participated in the vote (after excluding “Abstain” votes) need to have vetoed the decision (“No with Veto”).

If one of these requirements is not met at the end of the voting period, e.g. because the quorum was not met, the proposal is denied. As a result, the deposit associated with the denied proposal will not be refunded and instead be awarded to the community pool.

Phase 4: Implementing the Proposal

An accepted proposal will need to be implemented as part of the software that is run by the networks’ validators. There are many different types of proposals. Here are a list of the most important proposal types:

  • Text proposals: Text proposals contains a title and a description text. They cant be automatically implemented by the Blockchain. They can be used for nearly all kind of proposals. But to change some technically stuff on the blockchain other types of proposal maybe fits better. Like "Parameter change proposals".
  • Parameter change proposals: With this type of proposal parameters of the Unification Blockchain can be adjusted. For example: "max deposit phase duration". If such a proposal is successfull, the Blockchain will change the parameter automatically.
  • Community Pool Spend Proposal. This type of proposal is used withdram FUND from the Community Pool. The proposal contains the amount and receiver address. If such a propsal is successfull, then the Blockchain will withdraw the given amount automatically to the receiver address
